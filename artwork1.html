Firefiles on the Water

Illuminating the Infinite

Fireflies on the Water is a continuation of Kusama's ongoing investigation into the relationship between ourselves, the space we inhabit, and the enigmatic concept of infinity. Fireflies on the Water is a room-sized installation designed to be experienced alone, one person at a time. It is made up of a small, darkened room lined on all sides with mirrors, a pool of water in the centre of the space into which a dock-like viewing platform protrudes, and 150 small lights suspended from the ceiling. These elements combine to create a stunning display of direct and reflected light, emanating from both the mirrors and the water's surface. There appears to be no top or bottom, no beginning or end to space.
Like Yayoi Kusama’s earliest room-sized installations—including Infinity Mirror Room (1965), in which she combined mirrors and her signature polka-dotted phallic protrusions in an enclosed chamber—Fireflies embodies an almost hallucinatory approach to reality. While related to the artist’s personal mythology and therapeutic working process, it also refers to sources as varied as the myth of Narcissus and Kusama’s native Japanese landscape.
